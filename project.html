The Failure of the Skill India Mission and Building a Future-Ready Skilling Framework

Aims & Research Questions

Project Overview

India's demographic dividend‚Äîwith 60% working-age population and median age under 30‚Äîcreates unprecedented opportunity for growth. Yet 25% of youth are NEET, only 4.7% have formal vocational training, and youth unemployment reaches 30%. The Skill India Mission (SIM) trained 160 million but placement rates collapsed from 18.4% to 10.1%. This project examines why SIM failed and how curriculum alignment with domestic and international labor demand can transform outcomes.

Research Questions: (1) Why has PMKVY failed despite training millions? (2) How can curriculum alignment improve employability? (3) What reforms could deliver quality skills?

Conclusions

What the Evidence Suggests

The quantitative analysis reveals three critical findings. First, PMKVY's failures transcend state-level capacity differences‚Äîeven Gujarat experienced catastrophic placement collapse from 22.5% to 5.9%, suggesting systematic program design flaws rather than implementation issues. Second, government de-linked placement tracking from PMKVY 4.0 (FY 2022-23), eliminating accountability while claiming success based solely on training volume.

Third, machine learning analysis demonstrates that literacy and existing skill infrastructure predict placement better than GDP per capita, challenging infrastructure-heavy policy approaches. Combined with India's narrowing demographic dividend window and global labor shortages totaling 50 million workers by 2030, India could expand annual migration from 700K to 2.5M workers‚Äîbut only through fundamental SIM reform focused on curriculum-industry alignment, international certification standards, and quality-over-quantity metrics.

Methodology

Data Sources, Tools & Challenges

Primary Data Sources

  • PMKVY Performance: PIB Press Release PRID 2204132 (Dec 2025) providing state-wise training and placement data for FY 2020-21 through FY 2024-25
  • Demographics: World Bank World Development Indicators and UN Population Division (1990-2024 time series)
  • Labor Market: Periodic Labour Force Survey (PLFS) Annual Reports and India Graduate Skills Index 2025
  • Migration Data: GATI Report 2025, OECD Labor Market Statistics, Ministry of External Affairs
  • State Economics: Reserve Bank of India State Finance Reports and Census 2011

Data Challenges & Limitations

  • Placement De-linking: Government stopped tracking placement outcomes from FY 2022-23 onwards, eliminating accountability for the program's primary objective
  • State Name Variations: Inconsistent naming conventions across PIB annexures, census data, and geographic datasets required extensive manual matching
  • Missing Sector Data: Industry-specific placement breakdowns not published, preventing analysis of skill-demand alignment
  • Projection Uncertainty: Global labor shortage estimates vary significantly across sources; conservative GATI projections used

Analytical Tools & Methods

  • Python 3.x with pandas: Data cleaning, merging parliamentary question annexures across different administrative classifications
  • scikit-learn: LinearRegression for identifying state-level predictors, StandardScaler for feature normalization
  • Statistical Analysis: Difference-in-Differences methodology comparing Gujarat (advanced) vs Manipur (peripheral)
  • Visualization: Vega-Lite 5.x with interactive parameters including year sliders, metric selectors, and hover effects
  • Reproducibility: Google Colab notebooks with publicly accessible code and data

Research Design

  • Comparative Framework: Case study analysis of Gujarat (advanced industrial state with manufacturing clusters) versus Manipur (peripheral agrarian economy) to test whether state capacity moderates program outcomes
  • Theoretical Lens: Principal-agent theory for understanding policy implementation incentives; CIPP evaluation model for assessing inputs, processes, and outputs
  • Validation Approach: Triangulation of official quantitative data with qualitative stakeholder perspectives and international comparisons (Germany's Indo-German Green Skills programme)
