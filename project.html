Workforce Mobility and Youth Labour Outcomes in India: Examining Sectoral Employment and Skilled Migration Trends

Aims

Aim and Objective of the Project

India is in the midst of a demographic dividend, with roughly 60% of its population in the working‑age bracket (15–59) and a median age under 30. This demographic bulge presents us a window of opportunity to fuel rapid growth, but only if the young population are productively engaged. Yet nearly a quarter of the youth population is not in education, employment or training (NEET) and only about 4.7% of the workforce has formal vocational training. According to the India Graduate Skills Index 2025, youth unemployment hovers near 30%, with many graduates struggling to find jobs.The Skill India Mission (SIM) and its flagship Pradhan Mantri Kaushal Vikas Yojana (PMKVY) were launched to address this gap by training millions of young people. As of December 2024, SIM claimed to have trained more than 160 million individuals. However, the outcomes remain poor. PMKVY’s placement rate has stagnated at 18.4 % under its first phase, 23.4 % in the second, and just 10.1 % in the third. Understanding why SIM and PMKVY are failing and identifying reforms that link training to industry needs and international mobility are therefore urgent policy priorities.
